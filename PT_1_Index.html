Wi-Fi Standards

802.11 Evolution, Frequencies, and Use Cases

802.11b

Frequency: 2.4 GHz
Speed: 11 Mbps

The legacy standard. Slowest speed, high interference from microwaves/Bluetooth.

802.11g

Frequency: 2.4 GHz
Speed: 54 Mbps

Backward compatible with 'b', but faster. Still suffers from 2.4 GHz crowding.

802.11n (Wi-Fi 4)

Frequency: 2.4 & 5 GHz
Speed: 600 Mbps

First to use MIMO (Multiple antennas). The bridge between old and new Wi-Fi.

802.11ac (Wi-Fi 5)

Frequency: 5 GHz Only
Speed: 6.9 Gbps

Very fast, less interference. Introduced Multi-User MIMO for better concurrency.

802.11ax (Wi-Fi 6)

Frequency: 2.4 & 5 GHz
Speed: 9.6 Gbps

The modern standard. Uses OFDMA for high efficiency in crowded areas (stadiums, offices).

Laser Printing Process

The 7 Steps (Mnemonic: Please Come Eat Donuts This Friday Coming)

  • 1. Processing The printer receives the document and converts it into a bitmap image in memory.
  • 2. Charging The Primary Corona Wire applies a high negative charge (-600V) to the photosensitive drum.
  • 3. Exposing The laser shoots the drum, neutralizing the negative charge where the image should be.
  • 4. Developing Toner (negatively charged) is released and sticks ONLY to the areas the laser hit.
  • 5. Transferring The Transfer Corona gives the paper a positive charge, pulling the toner off the drum onto the page.
  • 6. Fusing Heat and pressure rollers melt the toner powder permanently into the paper fibers.
  • 7. Cleaning A rubber blade scrapes excess toner off the drum to prepare for the next rotation.

RAID Storage

Redundant Array of Independent Disks

RAID 0 (Striping)

Splits data across drives for speed. Zero redundancy. If one drive fails, everything is lost.

RAID 1 (Mirroring)

Duplicates data exactly. High safety. Requires 2 drives. You lose 50% of your total storage capacity.

RAID 5 (Striping w/ Parity)

Stripes data and "parity" bits across 3+ drives. Can survive one drive failure. Good balance of speed/space.

RAID 10 (Stripe of Mirrors)

Combines RAID 0 speed and RAID 1 safety. Requires 4+ drives. The most expensive but highest performance option.
